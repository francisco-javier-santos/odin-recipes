Grilled Cheese

Description

A grilled cheese sandwich is a classic comfort food that features two slices of bread, typically buttered and toasted to a golden brown, with a generous layer of melted cheese in between. The most common cheese used is cheddar, but variations often include a mix of cheeses like gouda, mozzarella, or Swiss.

This sandwich is often enjoyed on its own or paired with a bowl of tomato soup, making it a beloved choice for lunch or a quick snack.

Ingredients (for 2 people)

  • 4 slices of white bread
  • 3 tablespoons of butter, divided
  • 2 slices of Cheddar cheese

Steps

  1. Preheat a nonstick skillet over medium heat. Generously butter one side of a slice of bread. Place bread butter-side down in the hot skillet; add 1 slice of cheese. Butter a second slice of bread on one side and place butter-side up on top of cheese.
  2. Cook until lightly browned on one side; flip over and continue cooking until cheese is melted. Repeat with remaining 2 slices of bread, butter, and slice of cheese.
